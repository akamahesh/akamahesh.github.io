Intervention-  MedPANDA App

A randomized controlled trial to assess the effect of mobile health- based ‘MedPANDA’ app on symptomatic self- management and medication adherence among patients with leukemia at AIIMS, New Delhi.

DISCLAIMER-
MedPANDA is intended to provide supportive information and simple home remedies for symptom relief. It is not a replacement for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
Always consult your doctor for any health concerns or emergencies. Do not disregard professional medical advice or delay seeking it because of information provided by this app. Use this app as an adjunct tool only.

  • Feature 2:- Symptom management

  • Pain management –

⚠️ Seek Immediate Medical Help for Pain If:

  • Sudden or severe pain that is new or worsening rapidly
  • Pain not relieved by prescribed medications
  • Chest pain or pressure (could signal a heart or lung issue)
  • Severe bone or joint pain that limits movement
  • Abdominal pain with bloating, vomiting, or no bowel movement
  • Pain with fever, chills, or swelling (possible infection)
  • Headache with vision changes, confusion, or stiff neck
  • Burning or shooting nerve pain especially in hands, feet, or spine

🛑 Don't ignore persistent or unusual pain—get medical help promptly.

  • Diaphragmatic Breathing (Abdominal Breathing)

VIDEO

How to do it:

Sit or lie down comfortably.

Place one hand on your chest and the other on your abdomen.

Inhale deeply through your nose for a count of 4, allowing your abdomen to rise (not your chest).

Hold the breath for a count of 2.

Exhale slowly through your mouth for a count of 6.

Repeat for 5–10 minutes.

Benefits: Helps in focusing the mind and reducing anxiety, which can lower pain perception.

  • Pursed-Lip Breathing

            VIDEO

How to do it:

Inhale slowly through your nose for about 2 seconds.

Purse your lips like you’re about to whistle.

Exhale slowly through pursed lips for 4 seconds.

Repeat for 5–10 minutes.

Benefits: Enhances oxygen exchange, useful for people with chronic pain and respiratory conditions.

How to do it:

Hold your breath for 7 seconds.

Exhale slowly through your mouth for 8 seconds.

Repeat 4–8 times.

Benefits: Induces deep relaxation and lowers pain-related stress.

  • No food or fluid intake for more than 24–48 hours
  • Rapid weight loss or extreme weakness
  • Signs of dehydration: dry mouth, dizziness, low urine output
  • Persistent nausea or vomiting
  • Severe fatigue that prevents daily activities
  • Confusion or drowsiness
  • Fever or pain with loss of appetite (may indicate infection or organ issues)

🛑 Loss of appetite can lead to serious complications in leukemia. Get help if symptoms worsen or persist.

  • Nausea management

⚠️ Seek Immediate Medical Help for Nausea If:

  • Nausea lasts more than 24–48 hours despite medication
  • Repeated vomiting or inability to keep fluids down
  • Signs of dehydration: dry mouth, dark urine, dizziness
  • Vomiting blood or material that looks like coffee grounds
  • Severe abdominal pain or bloating
  • High fever along with nausea (possible infection)
  • Confusion, drowsiness, or extreme weakness

🛑 Uncontrolled nausea can lead to dehydration and poor treatment tolerance. Don’t delay medical attention.

Follow Dietary strategies like ;-

  • Snacking on dry crackers before rising
  • Eating small light frequent meals to aid digestion and reduce nausea.

   Follow behaviour and lifestyle adjustment like;-

  • Sitting upright after eating

  • Avoid combining hot and cold foods to prevent nausea.
  • Identify and track nausea triggers and avoid them.
  • Wear loose clothing to prevent abdominal pressure that might induce vomiting
  • Practice deep breathing exercises. Video 6

 

  • Foods to avoid:
  • Any food that’s fatty, greasy, fried, spicy, very sweet and has a strong odor
  • carbonated beverages like pepsi, coca-cola  can lead to vomiting in some people

   If the Nausea is severe then seek immediate medical attention.

  • Fatigue management-

⚠️ Seek Immediate Medical Help for Fatigue If:

  • Sudden or extreme fatigue that is unusual or worsening rapidly
  • Fatigue interfering with daily activities, like walking, eating, or speaking
  • Fatigue with chest pain, shortness of breath, or dizziness
  • Accompanied by fever, chills, or signs of infection
  • Pale skin, fast heartbeat, or shortness of breath (may indicate severe anemia)
  • Unresponsiveness, confusion, or fainting episodes
  • Fatigue not improved by rest or lasting more than a few days

🛑 Persistent or severe fatigue may be a sign of serious complications. Seek medical care promptly.

  • Details: Video

How it's done:

  • Sit comfortably with a straight spine.
  • Close your right nostril with your right thumb.

  • Close the left nostril with your ring finger, release the right nostril.

  • Details: Video

How it's done:

  • Sit in a comfortable meditative posture.
  • Take a deep breath in (inhalation is passive).
  • Forcefully exhale through the nose by contracting your abdominal muscles (exhalation is active).
  • Inhale automatically after each exhalation.

Usually done in rounds of 30–60 pumps per minute, followed by rest and deep breathing.

Benefits:

  • Detoxifies lungs and organs
  • Stimulates digestion and metabolism
  • Improves blood circulation
  • Energizes the body and mind
  • Clears nasal passages and sinuses

🌿 Basic Steps for Meditation

1. Choose a Quiet Space

Find a quiet, comfortable place where you won’t be disturbed.

2. Get Comfortable

Sit or lie down in a comfortable position. You can sit cross-legged on a cushion, in a chair with your feet flat, or lie down if you won’t fall asleep.

3. Close Your Eyes and Relax

Gently close your eyes and take a few deep breaths. Allow your body to relax with each exhale.

4. Focus on Your Breath

Pay attention to your breathing. Notice the sensation of air moving in and out of your nose or chest.
If your mind wanders, gently bring your focus back to your breath without judgment.

5. Use a Mantra or Visualization (Optional)

You can silently repeat a calming word (like “peace” or “om”), or visualize a peaceful image like a candle flame, a beach, or a light in your heart.

6. Be Present

If thoughts arise, observe them without attachment and gently return to your breath.
It’s normal for the mind to wander—just keep coming back.

7. Set a Time

Start with 5–10 minutes, then gradually increase as you feel comfortable. Use a timer if needed.

8. End Gently

When you're ready, slowly open your eyes. Take a moment to stretch, smile, and notice how you feel.

🧘‍♀️ Tips:

  • Be patient—meditation is a practice.
  • Consistency matters more than duration.
  • Morning or evening is often a good time to meditate.
