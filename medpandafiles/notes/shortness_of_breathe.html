Shortness of Breath Management

⚠️ Seek Immediate Medical Help for Shortness of Breath If:

  • 😤 Sudden or severe shortness of breath at rest or during minimal activity
  • 🫁 Breathing difficulty that worsens quickly or does not improve with rest
  • 💙 Bluish lips, face, or fingertips (cyanosis)
  • ❤️ Chest pain or pressure along with breathlessness
  • 😵‍💫 Dizziness, fainting, or confusion
  • 🚫 Inability to speak full sentences due to breathlessness
  • 🌡️ Shortness of breath with high fever, chills, or cough (may indicate infection or pneumonia)
🛑 These symptoms may signal a life-threatening condition. Get medical help immediately.

What You Can Do

1. Breathing Exercises

  • Diaphragmatic Breathing (Abdominal Breathing) Video
    • Sit or lie down comfortably.
    • Place one hand on your chest and the other on your abdomen.
    • Inhale deeply through your nose for a count of 4, allowing your abdomen to rise.
    • Hold the breath for a count of 2.
    • Exhale slowly through your mouth for a count of 6.
    • Repeat for 5–10 minutes.
    • Benefits: Focuses the mind, reduces anxiety, lowers pain perception.
  • Pursed-Lip Breathing Video
    • Inhale slowly through your nose for about 2 seconds.
    • Purse your lips like you're about to whistle.
    • Exhale slowly through pursed lips for 4 seconds.
    • Repeat for 5–10 minutes.
    • Benefits: Enhances oxygen exchange, helpful for chronic pain and respiratory issues.
  • 4-7-8 Breathing Video
    • Inhale through your nose for 4 seconds.
    • Hold your breath for 7 seconds.
    • Exhale slowly through your mouth for 8 seconds.
    • Repeat 4–8 times.
    • Benefits: Induces deep relaxation and reduces stress.

2. Positioning

Sit upright in a high Fowler or semi-Fowler position to promote lung expansion.

3. Improve Air Flow

  • Open a window for fresh air or use a fan near your face.
  • Avoid smoke, dust, and strong odors.

4. Avoid Overexertion

  • Stop and rest if you feel breathless during activity.
  • Break tasks into smaller steps.
  • Use a chair for support when bathing, dressing, or cooking.

If you experience extreme shortness of breath, seek immediate medical attention.

This app does not aim to replace any medical or professional advice.
